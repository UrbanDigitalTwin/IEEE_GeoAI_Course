About the Course

Designed for learning, research, and real-world disaster intelligence.

The course moves from core geospatial concepts to deployable AI workflows that support hazard detection, impact analysis, and decision-ready visualization.

🛰️

Remote Sensing Fundamentals

Work with raster data, multispectral imagery, SAR concepts, and preprocessing pipelines.

🤖

Machine Learning for GeoAI

Build supervised classification and deep learning workflows for geospatial intelligence.

🌊

Flood & Surface Water Mapping

Use satellite and terrain data to map flood extent and support emergency assessment.

🏙️

Urban Damage Assessment

Analyze buildings, infrastructure exposure, and flood-depth proxies in urban environments.

📊

Reproducible Pipelines

Convert raw open data into interpretable maps, metrics, and course-ready notebooks.

🌍

Open Data Ecosystem

Use Sentinel, OpenStreetMap, DEMs, and disaster datasets in accessible workflows.

Curriculum

Weekly notebook pathway

Each week is structured as a self-contained notebook with learning objectives, guided examples, exercises, and extension activities.

Core machine learning and computer vision concepts for disaster-management tasks.
